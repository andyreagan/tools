5% is a good all-around starting point. 3.5–4% in hot weather; up to 9% max for more calories (hypertonic — needs additional water to avoid intestinal distress).

Notes

  • Beta Alanine will make you "tingly" in the ears, forehead, etc. It enhances endurance performance — start 3 weeks before your race.
  • Citric acid: add a pinch to your bottle for flavor. A little goes a long way.
  • BCAA doesn't taste great; leave it out if off-putting. The benefit outweighs the taste, especially with a little citric acid.
  • Caffeine: 40–100 mg per serving, added per bottle (too small to mix evenly in a large batch).
  • Non-carb ingredients are negligible for concentration math — a 20 g scoop ≈ 4% in 500 mL.
  • Your body cannot absorb more than 90 g carbs/hour (60 g glucose + 30 g fructose). Don't exceed 1 L of 9% per hour.

Homemade Gel

  • Same formula as the Drink Mix — just use much less water.
  • Add a little bit of warm water at a time; usually half the amount of water to the amount of powder used.
  • E.g., if using ½ cup of drink mix, start with a little less than ¼ cup of water and keep adding small bits until you get the consistency you like.
  • It's amazing how much drink mix you can dissolve into a small amount of water…
  • These reusable dispensers are great for carrying your gels.

General Guidelines

  • Consume approximately one large bottle of fluids each hour during exercise — a light electrolyte drink with 10–40 calories per bottle.
  • Stick to liquid calories (gels are fine) during run training and running races.
  • Calories can be liquid or solid (chews, bars, real food) during cycling or swimming events.
  • Space out your target calories into 18–30 minute intervals.
  • No need to consume calories for workouts of 75 minutes or less.
